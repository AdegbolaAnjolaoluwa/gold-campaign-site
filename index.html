The Reality We Face

THE PROBLEMS
IN COLAW

01

Social Isolation & Burnout

Law students are buried under intense academic pressure with little to no organized social outlets, leading to burnout, anxiety, and disconnection from peers.

02

Weak Class Community

There is a lack of bonding activities across levels. Students from different years rarely interact, creating a fragmented and divided student body in COLAW.

03

Poor Event Planning & Execution

Past social events have been under publicized and poorly attended. Students feel events don't reflect their interests or celebrate their identity as law students.

04

Student Voice Not Heard

Students rarely have input into the social agenda. Events are planned without consulting the student body, leaving the majority unrepresented and disengaged.

Gold's Blueprint

MY SOLUTIONS
FOR COLAW

✓

Monthly COLAW Social Events

Structured, consistent monthly events eg. Inter level discussions, themed hangouts, music events designed to decompress law students and build real friendships across levels.

✓

Cross-Level Bonding Programs

Mentorship activities that connect 100-level students with finalists and higher levels to discuss/gist and rub minds about law, school and life in general.

✓

Student-Led Event Planning

A transparent event committee where students submit ideas and vote on social activities, ensuring every event truly reflects what COLAW students want.

✓

Actual Fun Events

Events that are actually fun and engaging, debate topics that get people talking eg Love or money?. Contests like a day in my life vlog for law students who love the internet as well as making videos etc
